# Mixed-Media Animation

<!-- analysis-date: 2026-03-27 -->
> **Reference data**: Analyzed 2026-03-27. Source accounts: @catpnut, amazon_yellow_teeth, hook_fb2, hook_fb5, burner_b006. Original @catpnut analysis date unknown. Amazon videos from content plan (likely early 2026).

Background visuals cycle between multiple rendered styles (3D anatomy, photorealistic AI scenes, recipe close-ups, product renders) while a speaker provides the voiceover. The speaker is either invisible (pure voiceover) or composited as a small PiP overlay in the bottom corner.

Two sub-variants exist based on whether a speaker appears on screen.

## Sub-Variants

### Variant A: Speaker PiP Over Rendered Backgrounds (Most Common)

AI-generated speaker sits as a small picture-in-picture overlay (bottom-left or bottom-right, ~15-20% of frame) while the full background cycles between rendered imagery. The speaker is consistent across the entire video (same outfit, same pose, same position). The background does ALL the visual storytelling. The speaker just provides a face for trust.

**References:**
- amazon_yellow_teeth (57s) — African healer PiP over 3D teeth renders + recipe close-ups
- hook_fb2 (57s) — Black female doctor PiP over 3D anatomy (skeleton, digestive system, blue background)
- hook_fb5 (67s) — African woman PiP over diseased organ renders + recipe B-roll
- burner_b001 (24s) — Elderly woman PiP over AI lifestyle/symptom imagery (short format)
- burner_b006 (121s) — African woman PiP over 3D ear canal + product bottle + phone mockup
- amazon_cracked_heels first half (0:00-0:38) — African healer PiP over cracked skin macro imagery

**Visual modes used in Speaker PiP variant:**

| Mode | What It Looks Like | When It Appears |
|------|-------------------|-----------------|
| 3D Medical Render | Giant anatomy (skeleton, organs, cross-sections), solid blue/dark background, clinical lighting | Mechanism scenes. "Here's what happens inside your body" |
| Photorealistic AI Scene | AI-generated realistic close-up of the problem (cracked heels, yellow teeth, fungal nails, earwax) | Hook and symptom scenes. Gross/visceral = scroll-stop |
| Recipe Close-Up | Photorealistic ingredients (salt spoon over bowl, mixing paste, jar being sealed, chopping herbs) | Instruction scenes. "Here's how you make it" |
| Product/Bottle Render | 3D rendered product bottle, supplement capsules in palm, packaging | Product reveal. "This is what I recommend" |
| Phone/Social Mockup | Phone screen showing Instagram post or Amazon listing | CTA scenes. Social proof |
| Abstract Internal | Artistic body interior (arteries, cells, flowing liquid, neural pathways) | Transition/bridge scenes. "Your body is healing" |

**Speaker PiP layout:**

| Element | Position | Size |
|---------|----------|------|
| Background visual (any mode) | Full screen | 100% |
| Speaker PiP | Bottom-left or bottom-right corner | ~15-20% of frame, seated, waist-up |
| Voiceover caption | Bottom third, centered (above or beside PiP) | 1 line, one key word highlighted yellow |

**Key details:**
- Speaker is AI-generated (not real footage) and stays in the SAME position, SAME outfit, SAME pose throughout
- Speaker is small enough that background imagery is clearly the focal point
- Speaker gestures subtly but never moves from their seated position
- Background imagery changes every 4-8 seconds, cycling through modes
- Yellow keyword highlight on captions is standard
- The speaker IS speaking (voiceover synced to lip movement) but the rendered background carries the visual weight

### Variant B: Pure Animation (No Speaker On Screen)

Multiple visual styles cycling with NO on-camera human. Voice is entirely voiceover (no visible speaker, no PiP). Each visual style serves a different narrative function. A recurring 3D animated character may serve as the emotional anchor.

**Reference:** @catpnut (105s) — educational listicle with 4 distinct visual modes alternating per content beat.

**Visual modes used in Pure Animation variant:**

| Mode | Purpose | Look | Frequency |
|------|---------|------|-----------|
| 3D Cartoon Character | Emotional anchor, personality, hook/recovery | Pixar-adjacent, semi-realistic, expressive, stylized environments | Most frequent. Hook, every other symptom, recovery |
| Photorealistic AI | Relatability, "this is you" symptom depiction | AI-generated realistic person, clean backgrounds, natural lighting | 1-2 per video, alternating with 3D character |
| Medical 3D Render | Authority, mechanism, science credibility | Cross-section anatomy, glowing particles, dark gradient backgrounds, clinical palette | 1-2 per video, at mechanism/explanation beats |
| Real Footage | Product trust, social proof, conversion | Actual filmed product, Amazon listing, review screenshots | Final 2 clips only. Hard cut from animation to reality |

## When to Use

- Educational/health content where the topic benefits from multiple visual registers
- Recipe/remedy content where ingredient close-ups are more compelling than watching someone talk
- Authority positioning through medical renders (anatomy, organs, cellular processes)
- Growth content where visual variety prevents attention fatigue across 60-120s
- When the topic spans visceral problem shots, scientific mechanism, recipe steps, and commercial product in one video
- **Variant A (Speaker PiP)**: When you want avatar trust + visual spectacle. Most Amazon-style content uses this
- **Variant B (Pure Animation)**: When no human face is needed. 3D character IS the brand. Faceless accounts

## Visual Structure

### Variant A (Speaker PiP):
```
[SYMPTOM/PROBLEM VISUAL: Gross/visceral close-up + speaker PiP]
    ↓
[RECIPE CLOSE-UPS: Ingredient prep, mixing, application + speaker PiP]
    ↓ cycles through recipe steps
[MECHANISM VISUAL: 3D anatomy or abstract internal + speaker PiP]
    ↓
[SPEAKER FULL-FRAME or continued PiP: CTA, "comment [keyword]"]
```

### Variant B (Pure Animation):
```
[3D ANIMATION: Recurring character — personality, emotion, hook]
    ↓ alternates per list item
[PHOTOREALISTIC AI: Relatable human experiencing the symptom/problem]
[MEDICAL 3D RENDER: Internal anatomy or mechanism explanation]
    ↓ repeats for each list item (3-5 items)
[3D ANIMATION: Character recovery — energized, relieved, transformed]
    ↓ hard cut to reality
[REAL FOOTAGE: Physical product + social proof (reviews, purchase screen)]
```

## Beat Structure

### Variant A (Speaker PiP) — typical 45-90s:
```
HOOK (3-8s):
  Background: Visceral problem close-up (giant yellow teeth, cracked skin, diseased organ)
  Speaker: Small PiP, delivering bold claim
  Text: Hook line, yellow keyword highlight
  Purpose: Scroll-stop through gross/shocking imagery

RECIPE/INSTRUCTION BODY (15-30s):
  Background: Cycles through recipe close-ups (ingredient in spoon, mixing in bowl,
    applying to skin/teeth/nails). Each step gets its own background image
  Speaker: Same PiP position throughout
  Text: Step instructions as captions
  Duration: 4-6s per recipe step
  Purpose: "Here's exactly what you do" — actionable, saveable content

MECHANISM/EXPLANATION (8-15s):
  Background: 3D anatomy, cellular processes, or abstract internal body visuals
  Speaker: Same PiP position
  Text: Science-sounding explanation
  Purpose: Authority. "This is WHY it works"

CTA (8-15s):
  Background: Product render, or speaker goes full-frame
  Text: "Comment [keyword]" / "Link in bio"
  Purpose: Conversion. Comment keyword for ManyChat funnel
```

### Variant B (Pure Animation) — typical 60-120s:
```
HOOK (5-10s):
  3D animated character in eye-catching close-up or action
  Purpose: Stop the scroll. Animation style IS the pattern interrupt

LISTICLE BODY (40-60s):
  Each list item gets its own visual mode, cycling to maintain novelty
  Structure per item:
    1. Numbered title appears (e.g., "1. Always Feeling Tired")
    2. Visual mode matches the content
    3. Voiceover explains the symptom + why it matters
    4. Duration: 8-12s per item

RECOVERY / SOLUTION BRIDGE (10-15s):
  3D character transforms — relieved, energized
  Purpose: Emotional payoff before the sales pivot

PRODUCT / CTA (15-25s):
  Hard cut to REAL FOOTAGE — physical product, Amazon listing
  Purpose: Trust escalation. Real footage after animation = "this is real"
```

## Text Overlay Style

- Single-word yellow highlight on the most impactful word per caption (standard across both variants)
- Clean sans-serif font, medium-large weight
- Consistent placement across visual mode changes
- Variant A: captions positioned around the PiP (above or beside, never covering the speaker)
- Variant B: two-layer system — top layer (list number/label) + bottom layer (voiceover caption)

## Camera

- N/A for 3D renders and medical imagery (these are generated assets, not camera shots)
- Photorealistic AI frames: front-facing, tight framing, clean lighting
- Recipe close-ups: overhead or 45-degree, tight on ingredients, shallow depth of field feel
- Speaker PiP: static, seated, waist-up, consistent angle throughout
- All 9:16 vertical
- No live camera work. All content is generated or rendered

## Pipeline Mapping

### Variant A (Speaker PiP):

| Component | Agent | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Speaker PiP image | Image Prompter | One image of the avatar seated, used across ALL scenes. Character reference for consistency |
| 3D medical renders | Image Prompter (photorealistic style) or external | Anatomy, organs, cellular. Prompt as "3D medical render" or use stock |
| Recipe close-ups | Image Prompter | Photorealistic ingredient close-ups. One per recipe step |
| Product renders | Image Prompter or filmed | 3D product bottle, capsules in hand |
| Compositing (PiP overlay) | Post-production | Speaker image composited over each background in editing |
| Voiceover | Veo Prompter (speaking) or voice clone | Speaker audio synced to PiP lip movement |
| Text overlays | Post-production | Yellow keyword highlights, step instructions |
| PatchWork | Partial | Background images go through pipeline. PiP compositing is post-production |

### Variant B (Pure Animation):

| Component | Agent | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| 3D animated character scenes | External tool | Pixar-adjacent 3D animation. Outside current pipeline |
| Photorealistic symptom scenes | Image Prompter + Veo/Seedance | AI-generated realistic person experiencing symptom |
| Medical 3D renders | External or stock | Cross-section anatomy, particle effects |
| Real product footage | Filmed manually | Product-in-hand shots, Amazon screen recordings |
| Text overlays | Post-production | Two-layer text system synced to voiceover |
| Voiceover | Separate recording | No on-screen speaking. TTS or voice clone |
| PatchWork | N/A | Most clips are external tools or manual |

## Key Differences from Other Formats

**vs. Talking Head**: Talking head has a full-frame speaker with occasional B-roll cutaways. Mixed-media has either no speaker (Variant B) or a tiny PiP speaker (Variant A) where the rendered backgrounds are the primary content. The visual variety IS the strategy, not the speaker's face.

**vs. Recipe Demo PiP**: Recipe Demo PiP shows real filmed recipe footage (hands chopping, blending, applying) with a speaker PiP overlay. Mixed-media uses AI-generated/rendered recipe imagery as backgrounds, and cycles through MULTIPLE visual modes (medical + recipe + product), not just recipe footage. Recipe Demo PiP is one visual mode. Mixed-media cycles 3-6 modes.

**vs. Text-Wall Over B-Roll**: Text-wall uses atmospheric footage as background with text as the narrative. Mixed-media uses purpose-driven visual modes where each style serves a specific function (symptom = photorealistic, mechanism = medical, recipe = close-up). Mixed-media treats visuals as the primary storytelling vehicle.

**vs. Green Screen Commentary**: Green screen has a visible speaker narrating over static evidence (screenshots, studies). Mixed-media either has no visible speaker or a tiny PiP. Authority comes from the rendered medical imagery, not from the speaker's credentials or the evidence being shown.

## Storyboard Adjustments

When planning this video type:
- **Variant A**: Plan one speaker reference image (avatar seated, consistent outfit). Then plan background images as a sequence: problem visual → recipe steps → mechanism → product/CTA
- **Variant B**: Plan the mode cycle in advance. Map which list items get which visual treatment before generating anything
- Each visual mode shift is a scene boundary
- Background images change every 4-8s (Variant A) or 8-12s per list item (Variant B)
- Flag which backgrounds are within pipeline (Image Prompter can do photorealistic renders, recipe close-ups) vs. external (3D animation, filmed product footage)
- No B-roll density setting for either variant. Visual variety IS the format
- Post-production is heavy: PiP compositing (Variant A), voiceover sync, text overlays, style transitions
- Total duration: 45-90s (Variant A, recipe/remedy style) or 60-120s (Variant B, listicle style)
