# Default Speaking Profile

Use this as the fallback when no specific profile is selected.

## Quick Reference
- **Profile ID**: `default`
- **Energy Level**: Moderate
- **Best For**: General UGC content, product demos, lifestyle content
- **Target WPM**: 150-170 WPM
- **Key Characteristic**: Natural, conversational delivery — authentic social media creator energy

## Voice & Delivery

**Volume & Intensity**: Moderate, conversational volume. Natural indoor speaking voice.

**Tone Quality**: Friendly, genuine, approachable. Not performative, not flat.

**Rhythm & Flow**: Smooth conversational flow with natural pauses. Neither staccato nor monotone.

**Vocal Techniques**:
- Natural emphasis on key words through pitch variation
- Conversational pauses at natural break points
- Avoid robotic or overly polished delivery

## Intonation & Pitch

**Pitch Range**: Mid-range with natural variation

**Pitch Patterns**: Conversational — slight rises for engagement, slight drops for authority. Natural statement intonation.

**Emphasis Style**: Through pitch variation and brief pauses, not volume spikes

## Emotional Quality

**Primary Emotions**: Genuine interest, mild enthusiasm, authentic engagement

**Attitude Toward Audience**: Friendly peer — sharing something useful, not selling

**Authenticity Level**: High — sounds like a real person talking to their phone

## Pacing & Timing

**Speaking Speed**: 150-170 WPM

**Sentence Length**: 12-18 words typical

**Pause Strategy**: Natural pauses between thoughts, brief pauses for emphasis on key points

**Silence Between Sentences**: Moderate (0.3-0.5s)

## Language Patterns

**Vocabulary Level**: Conversational, accessible

**Sentence Structure**: Mix of simple and compound — natural speech patterns

**Common Phrases**:
```
"So basically..."
"Here's the thing..."
"I'm not gonna lie..."
"This is actually really good"
"You guys need to see this"
```

**Filler Words**: Occasional "like," "so," "actually" — natural but not excessive

## Physical Performance

**Gesture Style**: Natural hand movements — pointing, showing, moderate gesturing

**Gesture Frequency**: Moderate

**Facial Expression Range**: Moderate — natural reactions, not exaggerated

**Eye Contact Style**: Direct but natural — looks at camera like talking to a friend

**Prop Interaction**: Natural product handling — holds, shows, demonstrates casually

## Technical Audio Specs

- **Stability**: Medium-High
- **Clarity**: High
- **Exaggeration Level**: Natural (minimal)
- **Vocal Fry**: Minimal to none

## Scene Segmentation Guidelines

All frames-to-video scenes are **8 seconds** (only supported duration with reference images).

| Duration | Words Per Segment | Calculation |
|----------|------------------|-------------|
| **8s** | 20-23 words | 160 WPM / 60 * 8 = ~21 |

Scenes with fewer words use visual beats (product pickup, posture shifts, reaction moments) to fill the remaining time naturally.

## Delivery Instruction Examples

**Example 1 - Opening Hook**:
```
DIALOGUE: "You guys need to see what I just found."

DELIVERY: Default - Natural conversational tone, moderate energy. Slight emphasis on "need" through pitch rise. Genuine excitement without shouting. "Just found" delivered with mild urgency. Direct eye contact with camera. Pace ~160 WPM.
```

**Example 2 - Demonstration**:
```
DIALOGUE: "So what you do is you just apply it like this, and honestly it works really well."

DELIVERY: Default - Instructional but casual. Slower pace (~150 WPM) during "apply it like this" for visual clarity. Natural breath pause after "this" (0.3s). Genuine approval on "works really well" — slight smile in voice. Moderate emphasis on "honestly" through brief pause before it.
```

**Example 3 - Call-to-Action**:
```
DIALOGUE: "I'll put the link below. You guys should definitely try this."

DELIVERY: Default - Warm, encouraging close. "Link below" delivered matter-of-factly. "Definitely" with gentle emphasis through slight pitch rise. Ends with friendly, confident tone. Pace ~155 WPM. Natural smile.
```

## Accent Recommendations

1. **General American** (default) — neutral, widely relatable
2. **Soft British (RP Light)** — adds slight polish without sounding formal
3. **Australian Casual** — adds warmth and approachability

**Default If None Specified**: General American

## Visual Synchronization Notes

**Hand-to-Speech Timing**: Gestures slightly anticipate key words (0.1-0.2s before emphasis word)

**Facial Expression Timing**: Expressions match emotional content naturally — no delayed reactions

**Critical Synchronization Points**: Product reveals, key benefit statements, CTA moments

## Profile-Specific Negative Prompts

```
monotone delivery, robotic speech, overly enthusiastic, fake excitement, infomercial style, whispered delivery, shouting, aggressive tone, professional narrator voice
```
