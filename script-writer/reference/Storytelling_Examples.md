## Category 1: Lifestyle Transformation HOOKS (March 10, 2026 — CURRENT PRIORITY)

**⚠️ These are HOOKS only — not complete scripts.** Each hook is 3 sentences designed to grab attention. After the hook, creators cut to a **podcast-style body script** (Category 3) for the actual product education, pitch, and CTA.

**Structure**: "It's been [X days/nights] now, and my [family member] still can't [common activity] because of [symptom]... Today we're finally trying that viral Rhodiola... And now she [does activity effortlessly]."

**How it works**: Show a real-life struggle with a common daily activity → introduce the product → show the "after" where the activity is effortless. The transformation is in **lifestyle quality**, NOT body parts. This bypasses TikTok's health-claim violations.

### Visual Rules (CRITICAL)

**Before scene:**
- Show the struggle visually from the **first second** — don't just explain it, make it visceral
- The physical **symptom must be visible in the frame** even while showing the activity struggle (e.g., arms or legs clearly framed)
- Use visual friction: crutches, leaning on counters, sitting down mid-task, rubbing legs in pain
- Lighting/mood: tired face, dark circles, dim or neutral lighting, exhausted expression

**After scene:**
- Show the **exact same activity** now done effortlessly
- Person looks energized, comfortable, happy
- Use warm/positive lighting: sunlight, golden hour, soft lighting
- **Never** show a direct before/after of a body part — only show the lifestyle improvement

**Visual Symptom Pyramid** (build from base to tip for maximum impact):
1. Show the symptom clearly
2. Make the symptom obvious
3. Add motion (pull, stretch, point at it)
4. Real person in a real place
5. High quality detail (close-up, high-fidelity)
6. Show real-life struggle (peak emotional resonance)

**What NOT to show:**
- No direct body-part transformations (this triggers TikTok bans)
- No weight-loss "after" photos
- Keep transformation focused on activity/lifestyle, not clinical results

**B-roll / dating glow-up variant:** When cycling through different before/after clips, the physical symptom must remain visible in the sequence (show arms in one clip, legs in the next).

## Category 8: Storytelling Hook Categories <!-- date: 2026-03 -->

### Wife Hero Journey (dramatic relationship hooks tied to aging/menopause)

Dramatic, provocative hooks that draw viewers in through relationship tension. The story follows the 6-beat framework (Pain → Pressure → Breaking Point → Discovery → Transformation → CTA). Target: women 35-55 dealing with menopause, aging, loss of sexual drive.

| Hook | Angle |
|------|-------|
| "My man cheated on me with a black girl" | Racial tension + infidelity |
| "My man divorced me because I looked too old for him" | Aging insecurity |
| "I didn't have sex with my husband for years so he cheated on me, now he can't keep his hands off me" | Sexual drive loss → recovery |
| "My husband just divorced me" | Post-divorce recovery |
| "I'm sleeping in my car tonight" | Rock bottom moment |
| "We have been sleeping in separate beds for around 5 years" | Long-term distance |

**Inspo accounts**: @misshealer_, @taimqbfjtr7

### Date Stories (regaining youthfulness)

Lighter, curiosity-driven hooks about dating and feeling young again. Same 6-beat framework. Target: women 40+ re-entering dating or reclaiming confidence.

| Hook | Angle |
|------|-------|
| "So I was on a date with a man twice my age" | Age gap curiosity |
| "So I went on my first date in 30 years" | Re-entry into dating |
| "So I am dating a guy half my age" | Youthful confidence |
| "So I never do this on a first date but..." | Open loop + taboo tease |

### Storytelling CTA Examples <!-- date: 2026-03 -->

These CTAs stay in story voice — they never break character to sound like an ad. Use these as templates for beat 6 of the 6-beat framework.

- "The brand is Salvora. It's in my shop. I'll update you."
- "I'm sitting here ordering something I'd never heard of three hours ago. Because I can't do this anymore. The brand is Salvora. It's in my shop."
- "I'm sitting in a parking lot at 2am ordering something I'd never heard of three hours ago."
