### SCHWARTZ ANALYSIS: Emotional Identity Hook

**Why This Hook is Powerful:**

**Relatable Entry Point:**
"Favorite jeans feeling tighter" is MORE relatable than:
- Medical symptoms (abstract)
- Death fears (too extreme for some)
- Lab numbers (impersonal)

It targets **identity through clothing**—a daily, visceral reminder.

**Pattern C Elements (Visual Proof):**
- Day 1 vs. Day 42 timeline
- Personal testimony ("changed my life")
- Before state implied (tight jeans)
- After state promised (fitting like they used to)

**Pattern A Elements (Symptom Recognition):**
- Multiple symptoms listed (waistband, thighs, hips)
- "That's cortisol" revelation
- Pattern recognition created

**Unique Schwartz Application:**

1. **Metaphorical Villain:**
   - Cortisol isn't just a hormone
   - It's "silently working against them"
   - Creates sense of betrayal by own body

2. **Hope Through Specificity:**
   - Not "lose weight"
   - But "jeans start fitting like they used to"
   - Specific = believable

3. **Lifestyle Contrast:**
   - "Exercise, eat healthy" (doing everything right)
   - "Still stuck in same jean size" (results don't match effort)
   - Creates urgency (effort is being wasted)

4. **Identity Restoration:**
   - "Feel good in your clothes again"
   - "Actually look forward to getting dressed"
   - "That light, confident feeling"
   - Not about appearance to others—about feeling like yourself
