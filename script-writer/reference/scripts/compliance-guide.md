# Compliance Guide: Safe Language for Health/Supplement UGC
<!-- date: 2026-03-11 -->

**TikTok only.** This guide applies when the sales channel is TikTok Shop. Amazon and Meta scripts do not need this compliance pass — they can be more direct with claims and language.

---

## 1. Safe Language Patterns

The goal: communicate product benefits without making medical claims. Three core techniques.

### Personal Experience Framing

Shift from universal truth to individual story. Platform algorithms flag claims — they rarely flag personal anecdotes.

| Unsafe (claim) | Safe (personal experience) |
|----------------|---------------------------|
| "This cures insomnia" | "I hadn't slept in weeks — now I'm out by 10" |
| "Reduces inflammation" | "My joints used to ache every morning. Not anymore" |
| "Burns belly fat" | "I noticed my waistline started changing after a few weeks" |
| "Lowers cortisol" | "I just feel... calmer. Like the stress doesn't hit the same" |
| "Fixes hormonal imbalance" | "Something shifted for me — my energy, my mood, everything" |
| "Eliminates bloating" | "I used to dread putting on jeans. Now they just... fit" |

**Key phrases**: "I noticed...", "For me...", "In my experience...", "I started to feel...", "What I can tell you is...", "All I know is..."

### Hedging Language

Softens claims without removing them entirely. Use when the script needs to reference a benefit directly.

| Hard claim | Hedged version |
|------------|---------------|
| "It works" | "It may support..." / "It's designed to help with..." |
| "Proven to..." | "Research suggests..." / "Studies have looked into..." |
| "You will see results" | "Results may vary, but many people report..." |
| "Guaranteed" | "If you're anything like me..." |
| "In just 7 days" | "Within the first couple weeks, some people start noticing..." |

**Hedging word bank**: "May help", "traditionally used", "many people find", "could support", "designed to promote", "often mentioned", "feel more [positive state]"

### Proxy Language

Replace medical/clinical terms with everyday equivalents that communicate the same idea without triggering filters.

| Clinical term | Proxy |
|--------------|-------|
| Weight loss | "Feeling lighter", "my clothes fit different", "waistline started changing" |
| Anti-inflammatory | "My body just feels less... angry", "things calmed down" |
| Hormonal balance | "Something shifted", "I feel more like myself" |
| Metabolism boost | "My energy changed", "I'm not dragging through the afternoon anymore" |
| Detox / cleanse | "Giving my body a reset", "cleaning things up from the inside" |
| Cure / treat | "Support", "help with", "formulated for" |
| Fat burning | "Body started changing", "things started tightening up" |
| Blood pressure | "My numbers improved", "my doctor noticed a change" |
| Anxiety relief | "I feel more at ease", "the noise in my head quieted down" |
| Pain relief | "I can move again", "mornings aren't a battle anymore" |

### The "People Find" Proxy Technique

Instead of making direct product claims, attribute to user experience:

- "Many people have shared how much better they feel..."
- "Users often mention their cravings feel more manageable..."
- "Customers typically report feeling more in control..."
- "People find it helps them feel less bloated..."

**Why it works**: "Many people" is vague enough to avoid substantiation. "Find" is subjective perception. "Feel" is emotional response, not medical claim. If flagged, defense is: "We're reporting what users say, not making medical claims."

### The "Comfortable" Reframe

For weight-adjacent claims, promise emotional comfort rather than physical change:

- Not: "Your belly will shrink" → But: "You'll feel more comfortable around your midsection"
- Not: "Lose 10 pounds" → But: "Feel more in control of your eating habits"
- Not: "Flatten your stomach" → But: "More comfortable in your own skin"

Still addresses the desire, still creates purchase motivation, but no body transformation claim.

## 2. Platform Risk Table

What triggers flags on each platform. Severity: LOW (rarely flagged), MEDIUM (sometimes flagged, depends on context), HIGH (frequently flagged or auto-removed).

### TikTok

| Trigger | Risk | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Direct health claims ("cures", "treats", "prevents") | HIGH | Auto-flagged by content filter |
| Before/after body-part photos | HIGH | Especially weight, skin clearing, body shape |
| "Doctor says" / medical authority framing | HIGH | Authority figure + health claim = double flag |
| Specific weight loss promises ("lose 10 lbs") | HIGH | Instant violation |
| Prescription drug names (tretinoin, GLP-1, Ozempic) | HIGH | Prohibited products |
| Skin bleaching/whitening product promotion | HIGH | Prohibited category |
| "Passing in their sleep" / death claims | HIGH | Extreme fear language triggers flags |
| Percentage claims without source ("65% more effective") | HIGH | Unsubstantiated claim |
| Measuring tape around waist (visual) | HIGH | Direct weight loss visual |
| Text overlays with health claims | HIGH | Text is OCR-scanned by platform AI |
| "Razor" + body part + directional movement (visual) | MEDIUM | Can trigger safety filter — use "grooming device" |
| Supplement mention + strong claim in same sentence | MEDIUM | Separate the claim from the product name |
| Symptom naming (bloating, inflammation, insomnia) | MEDIUM | Context-dependent — safer in personal story framing |
| "Body-shaming" language ("big gut", "fat arms") | MEDIUM | Can trigger body-image policy |
| Conspiracy language ("doctors hide this", "keeping you sick") | MEDIUM | Soften to "not widely discussed" |
| Historical/cultural narrative framing | LOW | Story format bypasses claim detection |
| Personal experience framing | LOW | "My experience" is much safer than universal claims |
| Activity-based before/after (Pattern T) | LOW | Shows activity transformation, not body parts |

### Meta (Facebook / Instagram)

| Trigger | Risk | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| Before/after weight photos | MEDIUM | Less aggressive than TikTok but still flagged |
| Direct health claims | MEDIUM | Flagged but enforcement is slower/less consistent |
| "Doctor says" framing | MEDIUM | Can trigger review |
| Supplement mention | LOW | Generally more lenient than TikTok |
| Personal story framing | LOW | Safe across all Meta surfaces |
| Historical/cultural narrative | LOW | Safe |
| Animated symptoms | LOW | Rarely flagged on Meta |

### Amazon

| Trigger | Risk | Notes |
|---------|------|-------|
| FDA-violating structure claims on listing | MEDIUM | Amazon reviews listing compliance separately from video |
| Direct health claims in video | LOW | Amazon is lenient on video content claims |
| Supplement mention | LOW | Expected — it's a supplement marketplace |
| Before/after visuals | LOW | Generally tolerated |
| Personal story framing | LOW | Safe |

**Summary**: TikTok is strictest. Meta is moderate. Amazon is most lenient. When in doubt, write for TikTok compliance — it will be safe everywhere.

## 3. Compliance Substitutions

Quick swap table. Find your unsafe phrase, use the safe alternative.

### Health Claims

| Treats [condition] | "Helps with..." / "Formulated to support..." |
| Prevents [condition] | "Designed to promote [positive state]" |
| Medical breakthrough | "Ancient remedy" / "What they knew centuries ago" |
| Clinically proven | "Research suggests..." / "Studied for..." |
| Doctor-recommended | "I did my own research and found..." |

## 4. Visual Compliance

What you show matters as much as what you say. Platform AI scans both.

### Do NOT Show

| Visual Element | Why It's Risky | Platform |
|----------------|---------------|----------|
| Before/after of body parts (waist, arms, face shape) | Flagged as body transformation claim | TikTok (HIGH), Meta (MEDIUM) |
| Measuring tape around waist | Direct weight loss visual | TikTok (HIGH) |
| Text overlays with health claims | Text is OCR-scanned by platform AI | TikTok (HIGH) |
| Weighing scales with numbers | Implies weight loss promise | TikTok (MEDIUM) |
| Medical imagery (pills in clinical setting, doctor's office) | Implies medical treatment | TikTok (MEDIUM) |
| Lab coats / stethoscopes | Medical authority visual | TikTok (MEDIUM) |
| Extreme close-ups of skin conditions | Can trigger medical content filter | TikTok (MEDIUM) |
| "Razor" + body part + directional movement | Triggers safety filter | TikTok (MEDIUM) |

### Safe to Show

| Visual Element | Why It's Safe |
|----------------|--------------|
| Person doing a daily activity easily (walking, sleeping, cooking) | Activity transformation, not body claim |
| Person holding/using the product in a lifestyle setting | Product placement, not medical claim |
| Personal story talking head / podcast framing | Narrative format, not claim format |
| Historical imagery / cultural scenes | Story framing bypasses claim detection |
| Product on a counter/table in a natural setting | Standard UGC product shot |
| Person smiling, energetic, living their life | Positive lifestyle, not medical outcome |
| Text overlays with personal language ("my experience") | Personal framing in text too |
| Electric grooming device (not "razor") in non-clinical setting | Safe term for close-up grooming scenes |

### Visual Symptom Pyramid — Compliance by Level

When showing symptoms visually (Pattern L), higher levels are more engaging but need more care:

| Level | What It Shows | Compliance Notes |
|-------|--------------|-----------------|
| 1-2 | Symptom shown simply/obviously | Generally safe — just showing a common problem |
| 3 | Motion added (pulling, stretching, pointing) | Safe if framed as personal experience |
| 4 | Real person, real place | Safe — grounds it in reality, not clinical |
| 5 | High-quality close-up detail | CAUTION on TikTok — can look like a medical image |
| 6 | Real-life struggle (can't walk, can't sleep) | Safe when framed as activity struggle, not diagnosis |

**Key rule**: Show the STRUGGLE with the symptom (activity-based), not the symptom itself in clinical detail.

## 5. CTA Compliance

Safe vs. risky calls to action by platform.

### TikTok Shop

| CTA Type | Risk | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| "If you still see the orange button below" | LOW | Visual anchor + soft scarcity — proven safe |
| "I'll leave the link right here" | LOW | Casual, UGC-native |
| "Tap the link below" | LOW | Standard, expected |
| "I want to apologize if it's sold out" | LOW | Removes sales pressure — compliance-friendly |
| "Once you scroll, you might not see this price again" | LOW | Availability scarcity, not health urgency |
| "Buy now before it sells out" + health claim | HIGH | Scarcity + health claim = aggressive flag |
| "Buy now before your condition gets worse" | HIGH | Health urgency is never safe |

### Amazon (Keyword CTA)

| CTA Type | Risk | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| "Comment [KEYWORD] and I'll send you the link" | LOW | Standard keyword CTA |
| "Comment [KEYWORD] — I'll DM you the recipe + the link" | LOW | Value-add DM, best practice |
| "Comment [KEYWORD] for the Amazon link" | LOW | Direct but safe |
| Health claim + "buy on Amazon" in same sentence | MEDIUM | Keep them separate |

### Meta Shop (FB/IG)

| CTA Type | Risk | Example |
|----------|------|---------|
| "Tap the shop button on this video" | LOW | Standard shop CTA |
| "Hit the product link right on this video" | LOW | Direct reference to button |
| Health claim + CTA in same sentence | MEDIUM | Keep them separate |

### Universal CTA Safety Rules

1. **Separate the claim from the CTA** — don't say "cure your bloating — tap the link." Say the personal experience first, THEN the CTA in a separate beat
2. **Scarcity is safe, urgency + claims is not** — "Small company, limited stock" is fine. "Buy now before your condition gets worse" is not
3. **"Once you scroll" urgency** — implying the deal disappears is safe. Implying the health problem worsens without purchase is not
4. **Apology frame** — "I want to apologize if it's sold out" removes sales pressure and reads as authentic, not aggressive

## 6. Risk Levels by Script Pattern

How much compliance attention each pattern needs. Higher risk = more careful language review before publishing.

### Patterns A-J (Short-Form Direct Sales)

| Pattern | Name | Risk | What to Watch |
|---------|------|------|---------------|
| **A** | Rapid-Fire Symptom Recognition | MEDIUM | Symptom list can trigger health content filter. Use personal framing: "Do you ever notice..." not "Symptoms of [disease] include..." |
| **B** | Command Structure | MEDIUM | "Take [product]" is directive — soften with "you might want to try" or frame as personal recommendation |
| **C** | Visual Proof Before/After | HIGH | Before/after body parts = instant flag on TikTok. Only safe if showing activity transformation (Pattern T overlay) |
| **D** | Misdirection Hook | MEDIUM | "The best cure for X is not Y" — the word "cure" triggers filters even in negation. Use "The best thing for X" |
| **E** | Medical Authority Frame | MEDIUM | "Doctors won't tell you" is provocative but functional. Avoid white coats, stethoscopes, or specific doctor claims in visuals |
| **F** | Taboo / Intimate Hook | HIGH | Naming intimate symptoms (incontinence, sexual dysfunction, body odor) can trigger content filters. Use euphemisms and personal framing |
| **G** | Category Redefinition | MEDIUM | "Everything is actually parasites" — conspiracy-adjacent framing is engaging but can trigger misinformation flags if too specific |
| **H** | Radical Negation / AAVE | MEDIUM | Dialect and style are safe. Watch for embedded health claims in the negation structure. Body-shaming language ("big ass gut") can trigger flags |
| **I** | Third-Party Empathy | LOW | Gift framing + third-party buyer = minimal health claim exposure. Safest of the A-J patterns |
| **J** | Niche Problem Reframe | MEDIUM | Connecting obscure symptoms to bigger health concerns can read as diagnosis. Frame as "I didn't know this was connected" |

### Patterns K-U (Extended Formats)

| Pattern | Name | Risk | What to Watch |
|---------|------|------|---------------|
| **K** | Pre-Hook | MEDIUM | Visual pain prime can be visceral — keep it activity-based (struggling to walk) not clinical (skin lesion close-up) |
| **L** | Visual Symptom Pyramid | MEDIUM | Levels 5-6 push into medical-image territory. Stay at levels 3-4 for TikTok safety |
| **M** | Podcast-Style Revival | LOW | Conversational delivery + personal framing = very safe format |
| **N** | Content Ratio Split | LOW | Growth content has almost zero compliance risk. Only the salesy 1/3 needs review |
| **O** | Keyword CTA + Funnel | LOW | The CTA mechanism itself is safe. Watch the script body for claims |
| **P** | Health History Conspiracy | LOW | Historical narrative bypasses claim detection. Keep historical "facts" vague enough to avoid fact-check flags |
| **Q** | Long-Form Personal Storytelling | LOW | Extended personal story = safest format. Product mentioned naturally, not as medical solution |
| **R** | Pain Point Animation / Personification | MEDIUM | Symptom personification ("I'm Belly Weight") names symptoms directly. Animation format adds a layer of safety but symptom naming still triggers some filters |
| **S** | Controversial Hook (New Accounts) | MEDIUM | Provocative hooks walk a line. If the "switch" resolves into a health claim, risk jumps to HIGH |
| **T** | Lifestyle Transformation Hook | LOW | Activity-based before/after is the safest transformation format. Currently #1 priority for exactly this reason |
| **U** | Dating Life / Glow Up Hook | LOW | Personal story + emotional driver. Safe as long as the "glow up" visual is lifestyle, not body-part close-up |

| Risk Level | Patterns | Compliance Action |

## 7. The AI Label Trick

**What**: Flash a dark screen or AI-generated face for 0.01 seconds at the very end of the video.

**Why**: This triggers TikTok's automatic "AI generated" content label. Having this label helps avoid content violation flags for AI-generated content on TikTok.

**When to use**:

| Sales Channel | AI Label Needed? |
|--------------|-----------------|
| TikTok Shop | YES — required for AI-generated content |
| Amazon (via keyword) | NO — not needed |
| Meta Shop (FB/IG) | NO — Meta has no strict AI-flagging penalties |

**How**: Add a single frame (0.01s) of either a solid dark screen or an AI-generated face at the very end of the final video, after the CTA. The viewer won't see it, but the platform's AI detection picks it up and applies the label automatically.

**When it applies**: Only for AI-generated content (AI avatars, AI voices, AI-generated visuals). Content using a real person filmed on a real camera does not need it.

## 8. Compliance-Optimized Script Example

Reference: Script #6 from the pattern library (Pattern D — Misdirection Hook, Rhodiola). This script demonstrates extreme hedging for maximum TikTok safety.

**Techniques used**:

| Technique | How It Appears |
|-----------|---------------|
| Tradition framing | "Traditionally used to help manage..." |
| Possibility hedging | "May help...", "Could support..." |
| Testimonial proxy | "Many people find...", "Users often mention..." |
| Feeling over outcome | "Feel more comfortable" instead of "reduces bloating" |
| No timeline | Zero time-based promises |
| Information sharing | "I share information about" instead of "I recommend" |

**The hedging structure** — every benefit claim follows this pattern:
1. Attribute to tradition: "Traditionally used to..."
2. Frame as possibility: "May help..."
3. Use testimonial proxy: "Many people find..."
4. Focus on feeling: "Feel more comfortable" not "reduces"

**Compliance rating**: VERY LOW RISK. Trade-off: slightly lower emotional intensity but consistent account safety and sustainable scaling.

## Quick Pre-Publish Checklist

Before any script goes live, scan for these:

- [ ] No "cures", "treats", "prevents" + medical condition
- [ ] No specific weight/body measurement promises
- [ ] No death claims or extreme fear language
- [ ] No prescription drug names or prohibited product categories
- [ ] No percentage claims without source
- [ ] All health benefits framed as personal experience OR hedged ("supports", "may help")
- [ ] Before/after visuals show activities, not body parts (if applicable)
- [ ] CTA separated from any health claims by at least one sentence
- [ ] No medical authority visuals (lab coats, clinical settings, stethoscopes)
- [ ] Text overlays use personal language, not universal claims
- [ ] AI Label Trick applied (TikTok Shop only, AI content only)
- [ ] Scarcity language is product-availability-based, not health-urgency-based
- [ ] Symptom mentions are in personal framing ("I noticed" not "symptoms include")
- [ ] Research products use approved hedged language ("studied for", "researched for")
- [ ] Intimate/taboo topics use euphemisms, not clinical terms
- [ ] "Razor" replaced with "electric grooming device" in close-up scenes
