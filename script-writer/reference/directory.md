## Schwartz Concepts (Quick Reference)

- **Market Awareness** (1-5): How much the prospect knows (1=unaware, 5=most aware)
- **Market Sophistication** (1-5): How many similar products they've seen
- **Mass Desire**: The pre-existing want you're channeling
- **Mechanism**: The unique "how it works" explanation
- **Intensification**: Amplifying the pain or desire
- **Identification**: Making the prospect feel "this is for me"

## Universal Script Elements

These appear in virtually ALL high-performing scripts regardless of pattern:

1. **"Orange Button" CTA** — "If you still see the orange button below" (visual anchor + scarcity)
2. **Apology Frame** — "I want to apologize if it's sold out" (removes sales pressure)
3. **"Months to Restock"** — "Waited for months" (future regret + pattern scarcity)
4. **Three-Part Quality Standard** — Lab tested + purity/potency + third-party verified
5. **"Once You Scroll" Urgency** — Implies discount disappears, FOMO trigger
6. **Small Company Story** — "Small company can't keep up with demand" (David vs Goliath)
7. **Heritage Authority** — "Used for thousands of years" (time-tested proof, bypasses FDA concerns)
